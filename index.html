Seattle: Landmarks & Neighborhood Change

A simple GeoNarrative for GEOG 458 Lab 7

This GeoNarrative uses scrolling text to guide a map through a few Seattle locations. Each scene triggers a new map view.

Author: Emmanual T. • Date: 2/19/26

Scroll down ↓

Scene 1: Downtown / Pike Place

Downtown is a major center of jobs, tourism, and transit. Pike Place is one of Seattle’s most well-known landmarks, drawing visitors from all around and supporting local vendors.

Scene 2: University of Washington

UW shapes the U-District through heavy student housing demand, transportation, and nearby businesses in the area. Large institutions can influence neighborhood change over time.

Scene 3: Rainier Valley (Columbia City)

Rainier Valley is known for its cultural diversity and community spaces. New development and transit can bring benefits, but can also raise affordability concerns for long time and new residents.
